## What's being tested

Six garrison subsystems landed without integration testing. Each has a specific behavior that should be observable in-game:

| # | System | Code |

## Setup suggestion

Pick a map with civilian/garrisonable structures (most maps have some). A skirmish vs an easy AI is fine — or 2-player local for cleaner control. River Zeta or any urban map is ideal. Bring at least:

- 4–8 infantry (mix riflemen + a heavy weapon if available)
- 1 supply truck (to test entering supply trucks works as expected, since CargoSupply replaced QuickRearm)
- Something with explosive AoE (rocket/mortar/artillery) to stress the suppression and damage pass-through

## Specific things to check (in priority order)

### 1. Indestructible buildings + damaged sprite
- [ ] Garrison a building with one rifleman. Hit the building with sustained fire from outside.
- [ ] **Expect:** building HP drops, but stops at 1 HP — never destroyed while garrisoned.
- [ ] **Expect:** at critical damage, the building's damaged sprite is showing.
- [ ] **Edge:** kill the soldier inside *first*, then keep firing on the empty building — should it now be destructible? Note what happens.

### 2. Dynamic ownership (enter → claim, empty → neutral, allies share)
- [ ] Find a neutral building. Send your infantry in.
- [ ] **Expect:** building's color changes to your player color; minimap reflects it.
- [ ] Pull all your infantry out (or have them die).
- [ ] **Expect:** building goes back to neutral.
- [ ] Send an allied unit in — both players' infantry should be welcome.
- [ ] **Edge:** what happens if your unit and an enemy unit try to enter simultaneously? (usually one wins; does the loser go elsewhere or get stuck?)

### 3. Directional port targetability
- [ ] Garrison a building that has at least one firing port. Note which direction the port faces.
- [ ] Position an enemy unit *in front* of the port. Order it to attack the soldier (force-fire if needed).
- [ ] **Expect:** enemy can target and damage the soldier.
- [ ] Move the same enemy *behind* the building (or to the side opposite the port).
- [ ] **Expect:** enemy can NOT target the soldier — only the building (and that's force-fire-only). Damage to the soldier should be near zero from outside the arc.
- [ ] **Edge:** AoE/explosion warhead — does the soldier take splash damage regardless of arc?

### 4. Suppression integration (duck @30, recall @60, lockout)
- [ ] Garrison a building. Hit it with sustained suppression-causing fire (MG, explosive nearby).
- [ ] **Watch the suppression pip on the soldier inside** — should rise.
- [ ] **Expect:** at >30 suppression, soldier ducks (doesn't fire from port).
- [ ] **Expect:** at >60 suppression, soldier is force-recalled out of the port (kicked out into the building's interior or ejected).
- [ ] Try to immediately re-garrison — **expect:** lockout for `SuppressionLockoutTicks`. Can't go back in for a moment.
- [ ] **Edge:** does the lockout apply per-soldier or per-port? If unit B tries to enter a suppression-locked port, does it get blocked?

### 5. Protection % text overlay + pips centered
- [ ] Visual: garrison pips render *centered at the bottom* of the building (not left/right corner).
- [ ] **Expect:** protection % text floats over the building, color-coded:
  - **green** when fresh / fully protected
  - **yellow** when degraded
  - **red** when nearly empty / no protection
- [ ] **Edge:** zoomed all the way out — is the % text still legible or just clutter?

### 6. Force-fire-only building targeting when garrisoned
- [ ] Select an enemy unit. Try to click-attack a garrisoned (enemy) building with a normal click.
- [ ] **Expect:** the click is ignored or treated as a move (building isn't auto-targeted).
- [ ] Try the same with force-fire (Ctrl-click or hold the force-fire modifier).
- [ ] **Expect:** unit fires on the building.
- [ ] **Edge:** does an *empty* (but recently garrisoned) building fall back to normal targetable behavior?

### Damage pass-through (cross-cutting check)
- [ ] Multiple soldiers in a building. Hit it with sustained damage.
- [ ] **Expect:** soldiers inside take damage proportional to incoming, but *port soldiers* take less (they have a DamageMultiplier from the `garrisoned-at-port` condition).
- [ ] **Edge:** soldier health bars should reflect this — port-side soldiers should die slower than building-interior soldiers.
